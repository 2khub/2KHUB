How to Plan the Perfect WWE Draft in Universe Mode

Published on March 21, 2026 • 8 min read

Planning a WWE draft in Universe Mode is one of the most exciting aspects of the game. Whether you're starting fresh with WWE 2K26 or revitalizing your existing save, a well-executed draft can set the foundation for months of compelling storylines and memorable moments.

Understanding Roster Balance

The key to a successful draft is maintaining balance across your brands. Each show needs a mix of Main Event stars, Mid Card talent, and enhancement talent to create realistic and engaging programming.

Consider the following distribution for a standard brand roster of 30-35 superstars:

  • Main Event (5-7 superstars): Your top champions and marquee attractions
  • Upper Mid Card (8-10 superstars): Contenders and secondary champions
  • Mid Card (10-12 superstars): Solid workers who fill out your weekly shows
  • Lower Card (6-8 superstars): Enhancement talent and developing stars
"A great draft isn't about getting all the top stars on one brand. It's about creating competitive balance that leads to unpredictable and exciting matchups throughout the year."

Draft Strategy Tips

When conducting your draft, keep these strategic principles in mind to maximize the quality of your Universe Mode experience:

Prioritize Star Power Early: Your first few picks should be established main eventers who can anchor your brand. Think Roman Reigns, Cody Rhodes, Rhea Ripley, or Bianca Belair. These superstars will be the foundation of your championship scene.

Don't Sleep on Tag Teams: Many players make the mistake of drafting individual superstars exclusively. Make sure to draft complete tag teams to maintain the integrity of your tag division. The Usos, FTR, and New Day should be kept together whenever possible.

Balance Gender Representation: Ensure both brands have a strong women's division. Aim for at least 8-10 female superstars per brand to sustain compelling storylines and championship feuds throughout the year.

Post-Draft Roster Management

After your draft concludes, take time to review your rosters and make any necessary adjustments. Use the 2KHUB Draft Tool to visualize your brand splits and identify any gaps in your roster composition.

Remember that Universe Mode is about creating your own wrestling world. Don't be afraid to make trades, call up NXT talent, or introduce custom superstars to fill specific roles your brands need.
